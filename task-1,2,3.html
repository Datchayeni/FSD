PLUMERIA

Plumeria (also called frangipani) is a tropical flowering plant known for its fragrant, waxy flowers. It is commonly grown in warm climates such as Hawaii, the Caribbean, and Southeast Asia. Its flowers come in white, yellow, pink, red, and multicolored varieties and are often used in leis and ornamental gardens.

Steps to Plant a Plumeria

  1. Choose a sunny location.
  2. Prepare well-drained soil.
  3. Plant the cutting or sapling.
  4. Water regularly.
  5. Watch it grow and bloom.

Features of Plumeria

  • Fragrant flowers
  • Tropical plant
  • Various flower colors
  • Used in leis
  • Easy to grow

Patchouli

Patchouli is a fragrant herb belonging to the mint family. It is widely known for its rich, earthy scent and is commonly used in perfumes, incense, cosmetics, and aromatherapy. The essential oil extracted from its leaves is highly valued for its calming and soothing properties.

Steps to Plant Patchouli

  1. Choose a warm, partially shaded location.
  2. Prepare well-drained, fertile soil.
  3. Plant patchouli cuttings or seedlings.
  4. Water regularly to keep the soil moist.
  5. Apply organic compost for healthy growth.
  6. Remove weeds around the plant.
  7. Harvest the leaves when the plant matures.

Features of Patchouli

  • Fragrant aromatic herb
  • Belongs to the mint family
  • Native to tropical regions of Asia
  • Used in perfumes and incense
  • Produces essential oil from its leaves
